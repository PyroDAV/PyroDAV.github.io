Learn the vocabulary

Knowledge of vocabulary is probably the biggest weakness of any russian-speaking belarusian. Although we understand most if not everything of the written belarusian language, speaking comes out to be very challenging to us. This could mean one thing: our active vocabulary is weak to various extents. To fix this issue, I created a list of new words that will be updated once in a while.

There is the list of new words that I have learned today

Зьнічка - падающая звезда/falling star

Прыступка - лестница/stairway

Агрэст - крыжовник/gooseberry

Пяшчота - нежность/tenderness

Досьвітак - рассвет/sunrise

What's up with literature?

Learning the vocabulary is a great way to remind you of the forgotten words and to get to know some brand new ones. However, vast knowledge of diverse words will not teach you to strand them into sentences and use in context. That's why reading literature is so important if you desire to improve speaking and sentence-building skills. There is the list of books and authors that I personally read and can account for.

This is the last book I have read in belarusian language

Svetlana Aleksievich's "Chernobyl Prayer" is dedicated to Chernobyl's technogenic catastrophe that happened back in 1986. The book is a collection of monologues of different people - liquidators, wives of already dead liquidators, migrants from "out there", settlers in "there", physicists, politicians, soldiers, elderly people, children collected over the past 20 years. Everything as it is, without editing, without "cuts"; it's all "their" truth, all "their" life and all "their" perception of this reality. Not only this book is thought-woking and egaging, it also contains enormous amounts of spoken belarusian language. This is rightfull masterpiece that should be considered by everyone.
